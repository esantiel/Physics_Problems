# 📘 Logic & Series – Fly and Bicycle Problem

A bicycle is 10 meters from a wall and moves towards it at a constant speed of $$1 \,\text{m/s}$$.  
A fly starts from the bicycle and flies towards the wall at $$2 \,\text{m/s}$$, bouncing back and forth instantly.

We are asked to find the **total distance the fly travels before the bicycle reaches the wall**.

---

## 🧠 Key Idea

The fly moves continuously the entire time until the bicycle reaches the wall.

So we use:
$$
\text{distance} = \text{speed} \times \text{time}
$$

---

## ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

### 🔹 Step 1: Time for the bicycle to reach the wall

$$
\text{time} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}} = \frac{10}{1} = 10 \,\text{s}
$$

---

### 🔹 Step 2: Distance traveled by the fly

$$
\text{distance} = \text{speed} \times \text{time} = 2 \times 10 = 20 \,\text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{20 \,\text{meters}}
$$
