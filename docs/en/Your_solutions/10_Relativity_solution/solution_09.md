# 9. Twin Paradox (ISS)

ISS speed:

$$
v=7.66\ \text{km/s}
$$

$$
v=7660\ \text{m/s}
$$

One year:

$$
t=365.25\times24\times3600
$$

$$
t=3.156\times10^7\ \text{s}
$$

For small velocities:

$$
\Delta t
\approx
\frac12
\frac{v^2}{c^2}
t
$$

Substituting:

$$
\Delta t
=
\frac12
\left(
\frac{7660^2}
{(3\times10^8)^2}
\right)
(3.156\times10^7)
$$

$$
\Delta t
\approx0.0103\ \text{s}
$$

## Final Answer

After one year the astronaut is approximately

$$
\boxed{0.010\ \text{s}}
$$

or about

$$
\boxed{10\ \text{milliseconds}}
$$

younger than their twin due to kinetic time dilation.
