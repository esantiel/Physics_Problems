# 10. Gravitational Time Dilation

Earth radius:

$$
R_E=6378\ \text{km}
$$

ISS altitude:

$$
h=400\ \text{km}
$$

Difference in gravitational potential:

$$
\Delta\Phi
=
GM
\left(
\frac1{R_E}
-
\frac1{R_E+h}
\right)
$$

Using the weak-field approximation:

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t}
\approx
\frac{\Delta\Phi}{c^2}
$$

Substituting Earth's parameters gives

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t}
\approx4.1\times10^{-11}
$$

Over one year:

$$
\Delta t
=
(4.1\times10^{-11})
(3.156\times10^7)
$$

$$
\Delta t
\approx1.3\times10^{-3}\ \text{s}
$$

## Final Answer

The astronaut ages approximately

$$
\boxed{1.3\ \text{milliseconds}}
$$

more than a person on Earth's surface because gravity is weaker at the ISS altitude.

Therefore:

- Kinetic effect: about 10 ms younger.
- Gravitational effect: about 1.3 ms older.

The kinetic effect is larger, so the ISS astronaut ends up slightly younger overall.
