# 8. Adding Velocities

Suppose one object moves at:

$$
v=c
$$

and another moves at speed:

$$
u
$$

The relativistic velocity addition formula is

$$
w=
\frac{u+v}
{1+\frac{uv}{c^2}}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v=c
$$

$$
w=
\frac{u+c}
{1+\frac{u}{c}}
$$

Multiplying numerator and denominator by c:

$$
w=c
$$

## Final Answer

No matter how fast the second object moves,

$$
\boxed{c+c=c}
$$

in relativity.

The relative speed remains exactly

$$
\boxed{c}
$$

which is why the speed of light is the same for all observers.
