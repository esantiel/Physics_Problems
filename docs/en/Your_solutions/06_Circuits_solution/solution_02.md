# 2. Resistors

We have exactly three resistors:

$$
R = 1\,\Omega
$$

We need all unique equivalent resistances using all three resistors.

---

## All Three in Series

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 1 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = 3\,\Omega
$$

---

## All Three in Parallel

$$
\frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = 1 + 1 + 1
$$

$$
\frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = 3
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{1}{3}\,\Omega
$$

---

## Two in Series, Then Parallel with One

$$
R_s = 1 + 1 = 2\,\Omega
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{(2)(1)}{2+1}
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{2}{3}\,\Omega
$$

---

## Two in Parallel, Then Series with One

$$
R_p = \frac{(1)(1)}{1+1}
$$

$$
R_p = \frac{1}{2}\,\Omega
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{1}{2} + 1
$$

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{3}{2}\,\Omega
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
\frac{1}{3}\,\Omega,\quad
\frac{2}{3}\,\Omega,\quad
\frac{3}{2}\,\Omega,\quad
3\,\Omega
}
$$
