# 15. Resistor Cube

A cube is constructed from 12 identical resistors.

Each edge has resistance:

$$
R
$$

We need the equivalent resistance between two opposite corners of the cube.

For a resistor cube between opposite corners, the standard result is:

$$
R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{5}{6}R
$$

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{R_{\text{eq}} = \frac{5}{6}R}
$$

---

## Explanation

Because of symmetry, some vertices of the cube have the same electric potential.

This allows the cube network to be simplified into series and parallel combinations.

The equivalent resistance between two opposite corners is:

$$
\boxed{\frac{5R}{6}}
$$
