# 7. Capacitors in Parallel

## Given

$$
C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}
$$

$$
C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}
$$

$$
V = 10\,\text{V}
$$

---

## Total Capacitance

For capacitors in parallel:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2
$$

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 4 + 6
$$

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\text{F}
$$

---

## Total Charge

$$
Q = C_{\text{eq}}V
$$

$$
Q = (10\,\mu\text{F})(10\,\text{V})
$$

$$
Q = 100\,\mu\text{C}
$$

$$
Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C}
$$

---

## Total Energy Stored

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(10)^2
$$

$$
W = 5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}
$$

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{Q = 100\,\mu\text{C}}
$$

$$
\boxed{W = 5.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}}
$$
