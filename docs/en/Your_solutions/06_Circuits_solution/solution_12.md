# 12. Transformer Currents

## Given

$$
N_p = 1000
$$

$$
N_s = 200
$$

$$
V_p = 120\,\text{V}
$$

$$
I_s = 3\,\text{A}
$$

---

## Secondary Voltage

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

$$
V_s = V_p \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

$$
V_s = 120 \frac{200}{1000}
$$

$$
V_s = 24\,\text{V}
$$

---

## Primary Current

For an ideal transformer:

$$
V_p I_p = V_s I_s
$$

$$
I_p = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p}
$$

$$
I_p = \frac{(24)(3)}{120}
$$

$$
I_p = 0.6\,\text{A}
$$

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{V_s = 24\,\text{V}}
$$

$$
\boxed{I_p = 0.6\,\text{A}}
$$
