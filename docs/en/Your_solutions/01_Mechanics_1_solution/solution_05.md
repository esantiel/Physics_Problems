# 5. Relative Velocity

A river flows **east** at

$$
v_r = 2 \text{ m/s}
$$

A boat can travel at

$$
v_b = 5 \text{ m/s}
$$

in **still water**.

The boat wants to go **directly north across the river**.

The river width is

$$
200 \text{ m}
$$

We want to determine:

1. The **direction (angle)** the boat must head.
2. The **time required to cross the river**.

---

# 1️⃣ Basic Theory

## Relative Velocity

When an object moves in a moving medium (like a boat in a river), the **velocity relative to the ground** is the vector sum of:

- the boat's velocity relative to the water
- the water's velocity relative to the ground

Mathematically:

$$
\vec{v}_{bg} = \vec{v}_{bw} + \vec{v}_{wg}
$$

where

- $\vec{v}_{bg}$ = boat velocity relative to ground
- $\vec{v}_{bw}$ = boat velocity relative to water
- $\vec{v}_{wg}$ = water velocity relative to ground

---

## Goal

The boat must move **directly north relative to the ground**.

That means the **east-west components must cancel**.

So the boat must aim **slightly west of north** to counteract the river current.

---

# 2️⃣ Velocity Components

River velocity (east):

$$
v_r = 2 \text{ m/s}
$$

Boat speed in still water:

$$
v_b = 5 \text{ m/s}
$$

Let the boat head at an angle $\theta$ **west of north**.

Then the components of the boat velocity are:

North component:

$$
v_{north} = 5\cos\theta
$$

West component:

$$
v_{west} = 5\sin\theta
$$

---

# 3️⃣ Cancel the River Current

The river pushes **east at 2 m/s**.

To go straight north, the boat must produce **2 m/s westward**.

So:

$$
5\sin\theta = 2
$$

Solve for $\theta$.

---

## Compute the Angle

$$
\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.4)
$$

$$
\theta \approx 23.6^\circ
$$

So the boat must head

$$
23.6^\circ
$$

**west of north**.

---

# 4️⃣ Speed Across the River

The northward velocity determines how fast the boat crosses the river.

$$
v_{north} = 5\cos\theta
$$

Substitute the value:

$$
v_{north} = 5\cos(23.6^\circ)
$$

$$
\cos(23.6^\circ) \approx 0.917
$$

$$
v_{north} \approx 4.59 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

# 5️⃣ Time to Cross the River

Time is

$$
t = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{velocity}}
$$

River width:

$$
d = 200 \text{ m}
$$

Thus

$$
t = \frac{200}{4.59}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.6 \text{ s}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answers

### Direction

The boat must head

$$
23.6^\circ
$$

**west of north**.

---

### Time to Cross

$$
t \approx 43.6 \text{ s}
$$

---

# 📌 Key Idea

To travel straight across a river:

- Aim **upstream** to cancel the current.
- Only the **perpendicular velocity component** determines crossing time.
