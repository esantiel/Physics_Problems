## 9. Momentum Comparison: Fly vs. Tennis Ball

Which has greater momentum: a 2-gram fly flying at 10 m/s or a 60-gram tennis ball moving at 1 m/s?

## 1. Basic Theory and Formulas

### What is Momentum?
In physics, **linear momentum** is a measure of the "quantity of motion" an object possesses. It depends on two factors: how much mass the object has and how fast it is moving.

### The Formula
The mathematical formula for momentum is:
    
**p = m * v**

Where:
* **p** is the momentum.
* **m** is the mass of the object.
* **v** is the velocity of the object.

### Units of Measurement (SI)
To properly compare values, we need to use standard SI units:
* Mass (m) is measured in **kilograms (kg)**.
* Velocity (v) is measured in **meters per second (m/s)**.
* Momentum (p) is measured in **kg·m/s**.

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Solution

First, we must convert the mass of both objects from grams to kilograms to ensure our units are consistent (1 kg = 1000 g).

### Object A: The Fly
* **Mass (m):** 2 g = **0.002 kg** * **Velocity (v):** **10 m/s**

**Calculation:**
> p = 0.002 kg * 10 m/s  
> p = **0.02 kg·m/s**

### Object B: The Tennis Ball
* **Mass (m):** 60 g = **0.060 kg**
* **Velocity (v):** **1 m/s**

**Calculation:**
> p = 0.060 kg * 1 m/s  
> p = **0.06 kg·m/s**

---

## 3. Comparison and Conclusion

Here is the summary of our calculations:

| Object | Mass (kg) | Velocity (m/s) | Momentum (kg·m/s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fly** | 0.002 | 10 | 0.02 |
| **Tennis Ball** | 0.060 | 1 | **0.06** |

### Final Answer
The **60-gram tennis ball** has greater momentum. 

**Why?** Even though the fly is moving 10 times faster than the tennis ball, the tennis ball is 30 times heavier than the fly. Since 0.06 > 0.02, the larger mass of the tennis ball easily outweighs the higher velocity of the fly, giving it a greater overall momentum.
