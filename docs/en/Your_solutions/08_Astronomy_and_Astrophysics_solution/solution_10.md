# 10. Measuring the Height of the Atmosphere

Given:

$$
R_E = 6370\,\text{km}
$$

Measured time:

$$
t = 40\,\text{minutes}
$$

Earth rotates:

$$
360^\circ
$$

in

$$
24\,\text{hours}
=
1440\,\text{minutes}
$$

---

## 1. Solar Depression Angle

Angular rotation rate:

$$
\omega
=
\frac{360^\circ}{1440}
$$

$$
\omega
=
0.25^\circ/\text{minute}
$$

After 40 minutes:

$$
\phi
=
0.25\times40
$$

$$
\phi
=
10^\circ
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{\phi=10^\circ}
$$

---

## 2. Atmospheric Height

Using

$$
\cos\phi
=
\frac{R_E}{R_E+h}
$$

Rearrange:

$$
R_E+h
=
\frac{R_E}{\cos\phi}
$$

$$
h
=
R_E
\left(
\frac{1}{\cos\phi}
-1
\right)
$$

Substituting:

$$
h
=
6370
\left(
\frac{1}{\cos10^\circ}
-1
\right)
$$

Using

$$
\cos10^\circ
=
0.98481
$$

gives

$$
h
=
6370(1.01542-1)
$$

$$
h
\approx98.2\,\text{km}
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{h\approx98\,\text{km}}
$$

This value is remarkably close to the commonly accepted Kármán line:

$$
100\,\text{km}
$$

which is often used as the boundary of space.
