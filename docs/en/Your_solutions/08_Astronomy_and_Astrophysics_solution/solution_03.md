# 3. Microgravity

ISS altitude:

$$
h=400\,\text{km}
$$

Distance from Earth's center:

$$
r=6378+400=6778\,\text{km}
$$

Gravity at this altitude is

$$
g=g_0\left(\frac{R_E}{r}\right)^2
$$

where

$$
g_0=9.81\,\text{m/s}^2
$$

Substituting:

$$
g=9.81\left(\frac{6378}{6778}\right)^2
$$

$$
g\approx8.7\,\text{m/s}^2
$$

## Why Are Astronauts Weightless?

Astronauts are not outside Earth's gravity.

They experience weightlessness because the ISS and everything inside it are in continuous free fall around Earth.
