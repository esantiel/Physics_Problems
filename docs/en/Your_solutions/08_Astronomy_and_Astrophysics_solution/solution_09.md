# 9. Aristarchus' Method

Given:

$$
\theta = 89.85^\circ
$$

$$
\alpha = 0.53^\circ
$$

$$
d_{EM} = 3.84\times10^5\,\text{km}
$$

---

## 1. Earth-Sun Distance

At half Moon:

$$
\cos\theta
=
\frac{d_{EM}}{d_{ES}}
$$

Therefore:

$$
d_{ES}
=
\frac{d_{EM}}{\cos\theta}
$$

Using

$$
\cos(89.85^\circ)
\approx 0.002618
$$

gives

$$
d_{ES}
=
\frac{3.84\times10^5}{0.002618}
$$

$$
d_{ES}
\approx 1.47\times10^8\,\text{km}
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{d_{ES}\approx1.47\times10^8\,\text{km}}
$$

---

## 2. Diameter of the Sun

Convert angular diameter to radians:

$$
\alpha
=
0.53^\circ
\times
\frac{\pi}{180}
$$

$$
\alpha
=
0.00925\,\text{rad}
$$

Using

$$
D_S \approx \alpha d_{ES}
$$

gives

$$
D_S
=
0.00925
\times
1.47\times10^8
$$

$$
D_S
\approx1.36\times10^6\,\text{km}
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{D_S\approx1.36\times10^6\,\text{km}}
$$

---

## 3. Diameter Ratio

Moon diameter:

$$
D_M \approx \alpha d_{EM}
$$

Since the same angle appears in both expressions:

$$
\frac{D_M}{D_S}
=
\frac{d_{EM}}{d_{ES}}
$$

$$
=
\frac{3.84\times10^5}{1.47\times10^8}
$$

$$
\approx0.00262
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{\frac{D_M}{D_S}\approx0.00262}
$$

---

## 4. Using 89.75°

$$
\cos(89.75^\circ)
\approx0.004363
$$

Thus

$$
d_{ES}
=
\frac{3.84\times10^5}{0.004363}
$$

$$
d_{ES}
\approx8.80\times10^7\,\text{km}
$$

### Answer

$$
\boxed{d_{ES}\approx8.8\times10^7\,\text{km}}
$$

The result changes dramatically because the cosine of an angle very close to

$$
90^\circ
$$

changes rapidly.

This shows Aristarchus' method is extremely sensitive to measurement errors.
