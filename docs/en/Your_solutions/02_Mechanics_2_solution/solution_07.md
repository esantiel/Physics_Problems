# 📘 Dynamics with Friction – Two Blocks System

A block of mass:

$$
m_1 = 5 \text{ kg}
$$

is placed on top of another block:

$$
m_2 = 10 \text{ kg}
$$

- A horizontal force:
$$
F = 45 \text{ N}
$$
is applied to the 10 kg block.

- The 5 kg block is **tied to a wall** (so it does not move).

- Coefficient of kinetic friction:
$$
\mu_k = 0.2
$$

We are asked to find the **acceleration of the 10 kg block**.

---

# 🧠 Theory

## Friction Force

Kinetic friction:

$$
f_k = \mu_k N
$$

Where:
- \(N\) = normal force

---

## Newton’s Second Law

$$
F_{\text{net}} = ma
$$

---

## Key Idea

The 10 kg block experiences **two friction forces** opposing motion:

1. Friction with the ground  
2. Friction with the 5 kg block (since the top block is held fixed)

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: Friction between 5 kg and 10 kg blocks

Normal force between them:

$$
N_1 = m_1 g = 5 \cdot 9.8 = 49 \text{ N}
$$

Friction:

$$
f_1 = \mu_k N_1 = 0.2 \cdot 49 = 9.8 \text{ N}
$$

👉 This acts **backward** on the 10 kg block.

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Friction between 10 kg block and ground

Normal force from ground supports both masses:

$$
N_2 = (m_1 + m_2)g = 15 \cdot 9.8 = 147 \text{ N}
$$

Friction:

$$
f_2 = \mu_k N_2 = 0.2 \cdot 147 = 29.4 \text{ N}
$$

👉 Also acts **backward**.

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Total friction force

$$
f_{\text{total}} = f_1 + f_2 = 9.8 + 29.4 = 39.2 \text{ N}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 4: Net force on 10 kg block

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 39.2 = 5.8 \text{ N}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 5: Apply Newton’s second law

$$
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m_2}
$$

$$
a = \frac{5.8}{10}
$$

$$
a = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answer

$$
a = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2
$$
