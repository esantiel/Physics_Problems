# 📘 Energy Dissipation – Bouncing Ball

A tennis ball is dropped from a height:

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

After each bounce, it **loses 30% of its mechanical energy**.

We are asked to find the **height after the second bounce**.

---

# 🧠 Theory

## Energy–Height Relationship

Mechanical energy at height \(h\):

$$
E = mgh
$$

👉 Energy is **directly proportional to height**.

So if energy decreases, height decreases by the **same ratio**.

---

## Energy Loss

After each bounce:
- The ball keeps **70%** of its energy (because it loses 30%)

So:

$$
E_{\text{new}} = 0.7 \, E_{\text{old}}
$$

Thus:

$$
h_{\text{new}} = 0.7 \, h_{\text{old}}
$$

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: After the first bounce

Initial height:

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

After losing 30% energy:

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: After the second bounce

Apply the same reduction again:

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Alternative compact form

We can also write:

$$
h_2 = (0.7)^2 \cdot 2.0
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.49 \cdot 2.0 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answer

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$
