# 📘 Harmonic Motion – Spring System

A mass oscillates according to:

$$
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
$$

Given:
- Mass: $(m = 10 \, \text{kg})$

We are asked to find:
1. The spring constant $(k)$
2. The total mechanical energy $(E)$

---

# 🧠 Theory

## Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

The general form of SHM is:

$$
x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)
$$

Where:
- ($A$) = amplitude
- ($\omega$) = angular frequency

---

## Key Formulas

### 1. Angular frequency relation

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

So:

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

---

### 2. Total Mechanical Energy

For a spring system:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: Identify parameters

From:

$$
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
$$

We read directly:

- Amplitude:
$$
A = 0.2 \, \text{m}
$$

- Angular frequency:
$$
\omega = 10\pi \, \text{rad/s}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Find spring constant \(k\)

Using:

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2
$$

$$
k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2
$$

$$
k = 1000\pi^2
$$

Approximate:

$$
\pi^2 \approx 9.87
$$

$$
k \approx 1000 \cdot 9.87 \approx 9870 \, \text{N/m}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Find total mechanical energy

Using:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1000\pi^2 \cdot (0.2)^2
$$

$$
(0.2)^2 = 0.04
$$

So:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1000\pi^2 \cdot 0.04
$$

$$
E = 20\pi^2
$$

Approximate:

$$
E \approx 20 \cdot 9.87 \approx 197.4 \, \text{J}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answers

- Spring constant:
$$
k = 1000\pi^2 \approx 9870 \, \text{N/m}
$$

- Total mechanical energy:
$$
E = 20\pi^2 \approx 197.4 \, \text{J}
$$
