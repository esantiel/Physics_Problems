# 📘 Energy & Momentum – Collision Problem

A block of mass:

$$
m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}
$$

slides down from a height:

$$
h = 3.0 \text{ m}
$$

It collides and **sticks** to another block:

$$
m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}
$$

(initially at rest).

We are asked to find the **final speed after the collision**.

---

# 🧠 Theory

This problem involves **two key physics principles**:

---

## 1️⃣ Conservation of Energy (before collision)

Since the track is frictionless:

$$
\text{Potential Energy} \rightarrow \text{Kinetic Energy}
$$

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

---

## 2️⃣ Conservation of Momentum (during collision)

Because the blocks stick together (perfectly inelastic collision):

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Note:
- ($v_2 = 0$) (second block at rest)

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: Find velocity before collision

Using energy conservation:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

Solve for ($v_1$):

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Apply conservation of momentum

Before collision:

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v_1 + m_2 \cdot 0
$$

After collision:

$$
p_{\text{final}} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Set equal:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Solve for final velocity

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answer

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$
