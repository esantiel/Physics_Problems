# 📘 Conservation of Energy – Pendulum Speed

A pendulum of length:

$$
L = 1.0 \text{ m}
$$

is released from an initial angle:

$$
\theta = 15^\circ
$$

We are asked to find the **speed of the bob at the lowest point**.

---

# 🧠 Theory

## Conservation of Mechanical Energy

For a pendulum (neglecting air resistance):

$$
E_{\text{total}} = \text{constant}
$$

So:

$$
\text{Potential Energy (top)} = \text{Kinetic Energy (bottom)}
$$

---

## Energy Formulas

- Gravitational potential energy:
$$
U = mgh
$$

- Kinetic energy:
$$
K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

---

## Key Idea

At the top:
- Speed = 0 → all energy is **potential**

At the bottom:
- Height = 0 → all energy is **kinetic**

So:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: Find height difference \(h\)

From geometry of a pendulum:

$$
h = L(1 - \cos\theta)
$$

Substitute values:

$$
h = 1.0 \cdot (1 - \cos 15^\circ)
$$

We know:

$$
\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.966
$$

So:

$$
h = 1 - 0.966 = 0.034 \text{ m}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Use energy conservation

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

Solve for \(v\):

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Substitute values

Take:

$$
g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 0.034}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{0.6664}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.816 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

# ✅ Final Answer

$$
v \approx 0.82 \text{ m/s}
$$
