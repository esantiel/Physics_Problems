# 📘 Inelastic Collision – Runner and Cart

A runner of mass:

$$
m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}
$$

moves at:

$$
v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}
$$

He jumps onto a stationary cart of mass:

$$
m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}, \quad v_2 = 0
$$

We are asked to find:
1. The **final speed** of the system
2. Whether **kinetic energy is conserved**

---

# 🧠 Theory

## Inelastic Collision

In a **perfectly inelastic collision**:
- Objects stick together after collision
- **Momentum is conserved**
- **Kinetic energy is NOT conserved**

---

## Conservation of Momentum

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

---

## Kinetic Energy

$$
K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

We will compare:
- Initial kinetic energy
- Final kinetic energy

---

# ✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

## 🔹 Step 1: Apply conservation of momentum

Since the cart is initially at rest:

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140)v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Compute initial kinetic energy

Only the runner is moving:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 9
$$

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Compute final kinetic energy

Total mass:

$$
m = 70 + 140 = 210 \text{ kg}
$$

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 4: Compare energies

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}, \quad K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

👉 Energy decreased.

---

# ❗ Conclusion About Energy

Kinetic energy is **NOT conserved**.

The lost energy is transformed into:
- heat
- sound
- internal energy (deformation)

---

# ✅ Final Answers

- Final speed:
$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

- Kinetic energy:
❌ Not conserved
