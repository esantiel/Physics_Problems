# 📘 Gravitational Dependence – Simple Pendulum

## 🧠 Theory

The period of a simple pendulum is given by:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Where:
- $(T)$ = period (seconds)
- $(L)$ = length of the pendulum (meters)
- $(g)$ = gravitational acceleration $(\text{m/s}^2)$

---

## 🔍 Key Idea

- The period depends on:
  - square root of length
  - inverse square root of gravity

So:

$$
T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}
$$

👉 If gravity decreases, the period increases.

---

# ✏️ Part 1: Period on the Moon

### 📌 Given

- Earth period:

$$
T_E = 4 \text{ s}
$$

- Moon gravity:

$$
g_M = \frac{g_E}{6}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 1: Use proportional relationship

$$
\frac{T_M}{T_E} = \sqrt{\frac{g_E}{g_M}}
$$

Substitute:

$$
\frac{T_M}{4} = \sqrt{\frac{g_E}{g_E/6}}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Simplify

$$
\frac{g_E}{g_E/6} = 6
$$

So:

$$
\frac{T_M}{4} = \sqrt{6}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Solve for Moon period

$$
T_M = 4\sqrt{6}
$$

$$
T_M \approx 4 \times 2.45 \approx 9.8 \text{ s}
$$

---

## ✅ Result (Part 1)

$$
T_M \approx 9.8 \text{ seconds}
$$

---

# ✏️ Part 2: Length for 1-second Period on Earth

### 📌 Given

$$
T = 1 \text{ s}
$$

We use:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 1: Solve for \(L\)

Divide both sides by \(2\pi\):

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Square both sides:

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Multiply by \(g\):

$$
L = \frac{gT^2}{4\pi^2}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Substitute values

Take:

$$
g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

$$
T = 1
$$

$$
L = \frac{9.8 \cdot 1^2}{4\pi^2}
$$

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Compute

$$
4\pi^2 \approx 39.48
$$

$$
L \approx \frac{9.8}{39.48} \approx 0.248 \text{ m}
$$

---

## ✅ Result (Part 2)

$$
L \approx 0.25 \text{ m}
$$

---

# 🎯 Final Answers

- **Period on the Moon:**

$$
T_M \approx 9.8 \text{ s}
$$

- **Required length for 1-second period:**

$$
L \approx 0.25 \text{ m}
$$
