# 4. Magnetic Torque

A rectangular loop of wire has dimensions:

$$
10 \ \text{cm} \times 5 \ \text{cm}
$$

The current in the loop is:

$$
I = 2 \ \text{A}
$$

The magnetic field is:

$$
B = 0.3 \ \text{T}
$$

The magnetic field is applied **parallel to the plane of the loop**.

We need to find the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop.

---

## Convert Dimensions to Meters

$$
10 \ \text{cm} = 0.10 \ \text{m}
$$

$$
5 \ \text{cm} = 0.05 \ \text{m}
$$

So the area of the loop is:

$$
A = (0.10)(0.05)
$$

$$
A = 0.005 \ \text{m}^2
$$

---

## Magnetic Torque Formula

The magnetic torque on a current loop is:

$$
\tau = NIAB\sin\theta
$$

where:

- $\tau$ is the magnetic torque
- $N$ is the number of turns
- $I$ is the current
- $A$ is the area of the loop
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength
- $\theta$ is the angle between the magnetic field and the normal vector to the loop

Since the number of turns is not given, we assume:

$$
N = 1
$$

---

## Determine the Angle

The magnetic field is parallel to the **plane of the loop**.

The normal vector to the loop is perpendicular to the plane.

Therefore, the magnetic field makes an angle of:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

with the normal vector.

So:

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

---

## Calculate the Torque

Using:

$$
\tau = NIAB\sin\theta
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\tau = (1)(2)(0.005)(0.3)(1)
$$

$$
\tau = 0.003 \ \text{N m}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop is:

$$
\boxed{\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3} \ \text{N m}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{\tau = 0.003 \ \text{N m}}
$$
