# 5. Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

We have a parallel-plate capacitor with:

$$
S = 0.02 \ \mathrm{m^2}
$$

$$
d = 5 \ \mathrm{mm} = 5 \times 10^{-3} \ \mathrm{m}
$$

$$
V = 500 \ \mathrm{V}
$$

We assume the space between the plates is air/vacuum, so:

$$
\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \ \mathrm{F/m}
$$

---

## 1. Capacitance of the Capacitor

For a parallel-plate capacitor:

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
C = (8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \frac{0.02}{5 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
C = (8.85 \times 10^{-12})(4)
$$

$$
C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \ \mathrm{F}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \ \mathrm{F}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{C = 35.4 \ \mathrm{pF}}
$$

---

## 2. Energy Stored in the Capacitor

The energy stored in a capacitor is:

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}(3.54 \times 10^{-11})(500)^2
$$

$$
W = \frac{1}{2}(3.54 \times 10^{-11})(250000)
$$

$$
W = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \ \mathrm{J}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{W = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \ \mathrm{J}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{W = 4.43 \ \mu\mathrm{J}}
$$

---

## 3. Electric Field Intensity Between the Plates

For a parallel-plate capacitor:

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
E = \frac{500}{5 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \ \mathrm{V/m}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \ \mathrm{V/m}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \ \mathrm{N/C}}
$$

---

## 4. Force of Attraction Between the Plates

The electric pressure between capacitor plates is:

$$
p = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2
$$

The force is:

$$
F = pS
$$

So:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(1.0 \times 10^5)^2(0.02)
$$

Since:

$$
(1.0 \times 10^5)^2 = 1.0 \times 10^{10}
$$

we get:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2}(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(1.0 \times 10^{10})(0.02)
$$

$$
F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \ \mathrm{N}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \ \mathrm{N}}
$$

---

# Final Answers

$$
\boxed{C = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \ \mathrm{F}}
$$

$$
\boxed{W = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \ \mathrm{J}}
$$

$$
\boxed{E = 1.0 \times 10^5 \ \mathrm{V/m}}
$$

$$
\boxed{F = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \ \mathrm{N}}
$$

---

## Note

In this solution, the symbol $V$ is used for voltage because the letter $U$ is sometimes also used for stored energy. The given value:

$$
U = 500 \ \mathrm{V}
$$

means the voltage across the capacitor plates.
