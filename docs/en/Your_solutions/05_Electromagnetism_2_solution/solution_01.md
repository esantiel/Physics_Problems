# 1. Gauss's Law

A point charge of:

$$
q = +2 \ \text{C}
$$

is located at the origin.

A spherical surface of radius:

$$
r = 1 \ \text{m}
$$

is centered at the origin.

We need to calculate the **electric flux** through the spherical surface.

---

## Gauss's Law

Gauss's Law states that the total electric flux through a closed surface is:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

where:

- $\Phi_E$ is the electric flux
- $q_{\text{enc}}$ is the charge enclosed by the surface
- $\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \ \text{C}^2/(\text{N m}^2)$ is the permittivity of free space

---

## Enclosed Charge

The charge is at the center of the spherical surface.

So the enclosed charge is:

$$
q_{\text{enc}} = +2 \ \text{C}
$$

---

## Calculate the Electric Flux

Using Gauss's Law:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.85 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

$$
\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The electric flux through the spherical surface is:

$$
\boxed{\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11} \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}}
$$
