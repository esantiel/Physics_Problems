# 7. Wavelength and Frequency

The human eye is most sensitive to light with wavelength:

$$
\lambda = 550 \ \text{nm}
$$

We need to find:

1. The color of this light
2. The frequency of this light

---

## 1. Color in the Visible Spectrum

A wavelength of about:

$$
550 \ \text{nm}
$$

corresponds to **green light**.

So:

$$
\boxed{\text{Color: green}}
$$

---

## 2. Convert Wavelength to Meters

Since:

$$
1 \ \text{nm} = 10^{-9} \ \text{m}
$$

we have:

$$
550 \ \text{nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \ \text{m}
$$

$$
550 \ \text{nm} = 5.50 \times 10^{-7} \ \text{m}
$$

---

## 3. Frequency Formula

For electromagnetic waves:

$$
c = f\lambda
$$

where:

- $c$ is the speed of light
- $f$ is the frequency
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength

Solve for frequency:

$$
f = \frac{c}{\lambda}
$$

The speed of light is approximately:

$$
c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## 4. Calculate the Frequency

Substitute the values:

$$
f = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{5.50 \times 10^{-7}}
$$

$$
f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \ \text{Hz}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The wavelength:

$$
550 \ \text{nm}
$$

corresponds to:

$$
\boxed{\text{green light}}
$$

The frequency is:

$$
\boxed{f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \ \text{Hz}}
$$
