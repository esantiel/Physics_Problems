# 2. Ampere's Law

Two long, parallel wires are separated by a distance of:

$$
d = 10 \ \text{cm} = 0.10 \ \text{m}
$$

Each wire carries a current of:

$$
I = 5 \ \text{A}
$$

The currents are in **opposite directions**.

We need to calculate the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

---

## Distance from Each Wire to the Midpoint

The point is exactly halfway between the two wires.

So the distance from each wire to the midpoint is:

$$
r = \frac{d}{2}
$$

$$
r = \frac{0.10}{2}
$$

$$
r = 0.05 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Magnetic Field Around a Long Straight Wire

The magnetic field produced by a long straight current-carrying wire is:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

where:

- $B$ is the magnetic field
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \ \text{T m/A}$
- $I$ is the current
- $r$ is the distance from the wire

---

## Magnetic Field from One Wire

Substitute the values:

$$
B_1 =
\frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5)}
{2\pi(0.05)}
$$

Cancel $\pi$:

$$
B_1 =
\frac{20 \times 10^{-7}}
{0.10}
$$

$$
B_1 =
2.0 \times 10^{-5} \ \text{T}
$$

So each wire produces:

$$
B_1 = 2.0 \times 10^{-5} \ \text{T}
$$

at the midpoint.

---

## Direction of the Magnetic Fields

Use the **right-hand rule**:

- Point your thumb in the direction of the current.
- Your fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field.

Because the two wires carry currents in **opposite directions**, the magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the **same direction**.

Therefore, the magnetic fields add.

---

## Total Magnetic Field at the Midpoint

Since the two fields are equal and in the same direction:

$$
B_{\text{total}} = B_1 + B_2
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2B_1
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2(2.0 \times 10^{-5})
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \ \text{T}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The magnitude of the magnetic field at the midpoint is:

$$
\boxed{B_{\text{total}} = 4.0 \times 10^{-5} \ \text{T}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{B_{\text{total}} = 40 \ \mu\text{T}}
$$

---

## Direction

The direction depends on the actual current directions.

For example:

- If the left wire carries current **upward**
- and the right wire carries current **downward**

then at the midpoint, both magnetic fields point **into the page**.

So:

$$
\boxed{\text{Direction: into the page}}
$$

If the current directions are reversed, the magnetic field points:

$$
\boxed{\text{out of the page}}
$$
