# 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)

A light ray travels from air into glass.

The refractive index of air is:

$$
n_1 = 1.00
$$

The refractive index of glass is:

$$
n_2 = 1.50
$$

The angle of incidence is:

$$
\theta_1 = 30^\circ
$$

We need to calculate the angle of refraction:

$$
\theta_2
$$

---

## Snell's Law

Snell's Law is:

$$
n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2
$$

Solve for $\sin \theta_2$:

$$
\sin \theta_2 = \frac{n_1 \sin \theta_1}{n_2}
$$

---

## Substitute the Values

$$
\sin \theta_2 =
\frac{(1.00)\sin 30^\circ}{1.50}
$$

Since:

$$
\sin 30^\circ = 0.5
$$

we get:

$$
\sin \theta_2 =
\frac{0.5}{1.50}
$$

$$
\sin \theta_2 = 0.333
$$

---

## Find the Angle

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.333)
$$

$$
\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ
$$

---

# Final Answer

The angle of refraction is:

$$
\boxed{\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ}
$$

---

## Note

Since the light travels from air into glass, it enters a medium with a higher refractive index.

Therefore, the ray bends **toward the normal**.
