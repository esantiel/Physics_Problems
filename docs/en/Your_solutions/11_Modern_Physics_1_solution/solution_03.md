# 3. Photon vs Electron

## Photon

Photon energy:

$$
E=\frac{hc}{\lambda}
$$

If wavelength doubles:

$$
\lambda \rightarrow 2\lambda
$$

then

$$
E\rightarrow\frac{E}{2}
$$

The photon energy is reduced by a factor of 2.

---

## Electron

For matter waves:

$$
\lambda=\frac{h}{p}
$$

Therefore:

$$
p\propto\frac1{\lambda}
$$

If wavelength doubles:

$$
p\rightarrow\frac{p}{2}
$$

Kinetic energy:

$$
K=\frac{p^2}{2m}
$$

Therefore:

$$
K\rightarrow\frac{K}{4}
$$

## Final Answer

Photon:

$$
\boxed{E\rightarrow E/2}
$$

Electron:

$$
\boxed{K\rightarrow K/4}
$$
