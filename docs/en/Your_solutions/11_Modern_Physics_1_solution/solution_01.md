# 1. De Broglie Wavelength

Electron kinetic energy:

$$
K = 150\ \text{eV}
$$

Convert to joules:

$$
K = 150(1.602\times10^{-19})
$$

$$
K = 2.40\times10^{-17}\ \text{J}
$$

For a non-relativistic electron:

$$
p=\sqrt{2m_eK}
$$

where

$$
m_e=9.11\times10^{-31}\ \text{kg}
$$

Thus:

$$
p=\sqrt{2(9.11\times10^{-31})(2.40\times10^{-17})}
$$

$$
p\approx6.61\times10^{-24}\ \text{kg m/s}
$$

De Broglie wavelength:

$$
\lambda=\frac{h}{p}
$$

$$
\lambda=\frac{6.626\times10^{-34}}{6.61\times10^{-24}}
$$

$$
\lambda\approx1.00\times10^{-10}\ \text{m}
$$

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\lambda\approx0.10\ \text{nm}}
$$
