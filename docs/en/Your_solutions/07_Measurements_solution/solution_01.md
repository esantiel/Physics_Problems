## 1. Propagation of Error I

The radius of the sphere is:

$$
r = (6.20 \pm 0.05)\,\text{cm}
$$

The volume of a sphere is given by:

$$
V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
$$

Substituting the measured radius:

$$
V = \frac{4}{3}\pi (6.20)^3
$$

$$
V \approx \frac{4}{3}\pi (238.328)
$$

$$
V \approx 998.31 \,\text{cm}^3
$$

---

### Uncertainty Calculation

For a quantity of the form

$$
V = Cr^n
$$

the relative uncertainty is

$$
\frac{\Delta V}{V} = n\frac{\Delta r}{r}
$$

Since

$$
V \propto r^3,
$$

we have

$$
\frac{\Delta V}{V} = 3\frac{\Delta r}{r}
$$

Substituting the values:

$$
\frac{\Delta V}{V}
= 3\left(\frac{0.05}{6.20}\right)
\approx 0.02419
$$

Now calculate the absolute uncertainty:

$$
\Delta V
= V\left(\frac{\Delta V}{V}\right)
$$

$$
\Delta V
= 998.31(0.02419)
$$

$$
\Delta V \approx 24.15 \,\text{cm}^3
$$

Rounding the uncertainty to two significant figures:

$$
\Delta V \approx 24 \,\text{cm}^3
$$

and rounding the volume to the same place value:

$$
V \approx 998 \,\text{cm}^3
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
V = (998 \pm 24)\,\text{cm}^3
$$
