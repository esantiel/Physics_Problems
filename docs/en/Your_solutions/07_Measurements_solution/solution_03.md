## 3. Propagation of Error III

The voltage and current are measured as:

$$V = (10.0 \pm 0.2)\,\text{V}$$

$$I = (2.00 \pm 0.05)\,\text{A}$$

The resistance is calculated using Ohm's Law:

$$R = \frac{V}{I}$$

Substituting the measured values:

$$R = \frac{10.0}{2.00}$$

$$R = 5.00\,\Omega$$

---

### Uncertainty Calculation

For division, the relative uncertainties add:

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = \frac{\Delta V}{V} + \frac{\Delta I}{I}$$

Substituting the values:

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = \frac{0.2}{10.0} + \frac{0.05}{2.00}$$

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = 0.020 + 0.025$$

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = 0.045$$

The absolute uncertainty is:

$$\Delta R = R \times \frac{\Delta R}{R}$$

$$\Delta R = 5.00 \times 0.045$$

$$\Delta R = 0.225\,\Omega$$

Rounding the uncertainty to two significant figures:

$$\Delta R \approx 0.23\,\Omega$$

---

## Final Answer

$$R = (5.00 \pm 0.23)\,\Omega$$
