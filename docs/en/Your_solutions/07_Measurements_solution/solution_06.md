## 6. Instrument Precision

The thermometer reading is:

$$
T = 25.4^\circ\text{C}
$$

The last digit is in the **tenths place**:

$$
0.1^\circ\text{C}
$$

For a digital instrument, the uncertainty is typically taken as **half of the smallest displayed increment**:

$$
\Delta T = \frac{0.1^\circ\text{C}}{2}
$$

$$
\Delta T = 0.05^\circ\text{C}
$$

Therefore, the measurement can be written as:

$$
T = (25.4 \pm 0.05)^\circ\text{C}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\Delta T = 0.05^\circ\text{C}}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{T = (25.4 \pm 0.05)^\circ\text{C}}
$$
