## 4. Relative Uncertainty

The speedometer reading is:

$$
v = 60 \,\text{km/h}
$$

The relative uncertainty is:

$$
5\% = 0.05
$$

Calculate the absolute uncertainty:

$$
\Delta v = 0.05 \times 60
$$

$$
\Delta v = 3 \,\text{km/h}
$$

Therefore, the speed is:

$$
v = (60 \pm 3)\,\text{km/h}
$$

---

### Range of Actual Speed

Minimum speed:

$$
v_{\min} = 60 - 3 = 57 \,\text{km/h}
$$

Maximum speed:

$$
v_{\max} = 60 + 3 = 63 \,\text{km/h}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
v = (60 \pm 3)\,\text{km/h}
$$

Therefore, the car's actual speed is in the range

$$
57 \,\text{km/h} \leq v \leq 63 \,\text{km/h}
$$
