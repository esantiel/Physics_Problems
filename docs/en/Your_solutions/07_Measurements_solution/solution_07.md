## 7. Standard Deviation

The test scores are:

$$
88,\;92,\;79,\;85,\;95,\;81,\;86,\;90,\;83,\;77,\;89
$$

Number of scores:

$$
N = 11
$$

---

### Step 1: Calculate the Mean

The mean is:

$$
\bar{x}=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}x_i
$$

First, find the sum of all scores:

$$
88+92+79+85+95+81+86+90+83+77+89=945
$$

Therefore,

$$
\bar{x}=\frac{945}{11}
$$

$$
\bar{x}=85.91
$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate the Standard Deviation

Using

$$
\sigma=\sqrt{\frac{1}{N-1}\sum_{i=1}^{N}(x_i-\bar{x})^2}
$$

Compute the squared deviations:

| Score | $(x_i-\bar{x})^2$ |
|---------|---------|
| 88 | 4.37 |
| 92 | 37.10 |
| 79 | 47.75 |
| 85 | 0.83 |
| 95 | 82.64 |
| 81 | 24.17 |
| 86 | 0.01 |
| 90 | 16.74 |
| 83 | 8.45 |
| 77 | 79.37 |
| 89 | 9.55 |

Sum:

$$
\sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2 = 310.98
$$

Then

$$
\sigma=\sqrt{\frac{310.98}{10}}
$$

$$
\sigma=\sqrt{31.10}
$$

$$
\sigma \approx 5.58
$$

---

## Remove the Highest and Lowest Scores

$$

---

### New Mean

Sum:

$$
88+92+79+85+81+86+90+83+89=773
$$

Therefore,

$$
\bar{x}=\frac{773}{9}
$$

$$
\bar{x}=85.89
$$

---

### New Standard Deviation

Compute squared deviations from the new mean and sum them:

$$
\sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2 = 142.89
$$

Then

$$
\sigma
=
\sqrt{\frac{142.89}{8}}
$$

$$
\sigma
=
\sqrt{17.86}
$$

$$
\sigma \approx 4.23
$$
