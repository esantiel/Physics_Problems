# 1. Quantum Numbers

For the principal quantum number

$$
n=3
$$

the orbital quantum number can take values

$$
l=0,1,2
$$

For each value of \(l\):

### \(l=0\)

$$
m_l=0
$$

Number of states:

$$
1
$$

### \(l=1\)

$$
m_l=-1,0,+1
$$

Number of states:

$$
3
$$

### \(l=2\)

$$
m_l=-2,-1,0,+1,+2
$$

Number of states:

$$
5
$$

Total orbital states:

$$
1+3+5=9
$$

Including electron spin:

$$
2(9)=18
$$

## Final Answer

Possible values:

$$
\boxed{l=0,1,2}
$$

Possible magnetic quantum numbers:

$$
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
l=0 &: m_l=0\\
l=1 &: m_l=-1,0,+1\\
l=2 &: m_l=-2,-1,0,+1,+2
\end{aligned}
}
$$

Number of spatial states:

$$
\boxed{9}
$$

Including spin:

$$
\boxed{18}
$$
