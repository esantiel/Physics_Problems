# 7. Cyclotron Motion

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of:

$$
V = 5000 \ \text{V}
$$

Then it enters a uniform magnetic field:

$$
B = 0.1 \ \text{T}
$$

The magnetic field is perpendicular to the electron's velocity.

We need to find the radius of the circular path.

---

## Given Values

Charge of electron:

$$
e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \ \text{C}
$$

Mass of electron:

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \ \text{kg}
$$

Potential difference:

$$
V = 5000 \ \text{V}
$$

Magnetic field:

$$
B = 0.1 \ \text{T}
$$

---

# 1. Find the Speed of the Electron

When the electron is accelerated through a potential difference, electric potential energy becomes kinetic energy:

$$
eV = \frac{1}{2}m_ev^2
$$

Solve for velocity:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v =
\sqrt{
\frac{2(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(5000)}
{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}
}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \ \text{m/s}
$$

---

# 2. Magnetic Force Provides Centripetal Force

When the electron enters the magnetic field, the magnetic force causes circular motion.

The magnetic force is:

$$
F_B = evB
$$

The centripetal force is:

$$
F_c = \frac{m_ev^2}{r}
$$

For circular motion:

$$
evB = \frac{m_ev^2}{r}
$$

Cancel one $v$:

$$
eB = \frac{m_ev}{r}
$$

Solve for $r$:

$$
r = \frac{m_ev}{eB}
$$

---

# 3. Calculate the Radius

Substitute the values:

$$
r =
\frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)}
{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \ \text{m}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The radius of the circular path is:

$$
\boxed{r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \ \text{m}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{r \approx 2.38 \ \text{mm}}
$$

---

## Conclusion

The electron will move in a circular path with a radius of approximately:

$$
\boxed{2.38 \ \text{mm}}
$$
