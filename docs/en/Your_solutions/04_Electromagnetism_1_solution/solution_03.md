# 3. Electrostatic Equilibrium

We are given two positive charges:

$$
q_1 = +4 \ \text{C}
$$

$$
q_2 = +9 \ \text{C}
$$

They are separated by a distance of:

$$
d = 2 \ \text{m}
$$

A third charge is placed on the line between them:

$$
q_3 = +1 \ \text{C}
$$

We need to find the position where the net electric force on $q_3$ is zero.

---

## Important Idea

Since all charges are positive, the forces are **repulsive**.

So:

- $q_1$ pushes $q_3$ away from $q_1$
- $q_2$ pushes $q_3$ away from $q_2$

Between the two charges, these forces act in opposite directions.

Therefore, equilibrium is possible somewhere between $q_1$ and $q_2$.

---

## Define the Position

Let the distance from $q_1$ to $q_3$ be:

$$
x
$$

Then the distance from $q_3$ to $q_2$ is:

$$
2 - x
$$

So:

$$
r_1 = x
$$

$$
r_2 = 2 - x
$$

---

## Coulomb's Law

The electric force is:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}
$$

For equilibrium, the force from $q_1$ must equal the force from $q_2$:

$$
F_1 = F_2
$$

So:

$$
k \frac{|q_1q_3|}{x^2}
=
k \frac{|q_2q_3|}{(2-x)^2}
$$

Cancel $k$ and $q_3$:

$$
\frac{q_1}{x^2}
=
\frac{q_2}{(2-x)^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\frac{4}{x^2}
=
\frac{9}{(2-x)^2}
$$

---

## Solve for $x$

Take the square root of both sides:

$$
\frac{2}{x}
=
\frac{3}{2-x}
$$

Cross multiply:

$$
2(2-x) = 3x
$$

$$
4 - 2x = 3x
$$

$$
4 = 5x
$$

$$
x = 0.8 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Check the Distance from $q_2$

Since the total distance is 2 m:

$$
2 - x = 2 - 0.8
$$

$$
2 - x = 1.2 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Final Answer

The equilibrium position is:

$$
\boxed{0.8 \ \text{m from } q_1}
$$

or equivalently:

$$
\boxed{1.2 \ \text{m from } q_2}
$$

---

## Explanation

The equilibrium point is closer to the smaller charge, $q_1 = +4 \ \text{C}$.

This makes sense because $q_2 = +9 \ \text{C}$ is stronger, so $q_3$ must be farther from $q_2$ for the forces to balance.
