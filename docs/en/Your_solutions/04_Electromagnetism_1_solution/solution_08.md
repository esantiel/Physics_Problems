# 8. Lorentz Force

A charged particle enters a magnetic field perpendicular to the field.

We are given:

$$
q = 2 \times 10^{-19} \ \text{C}
$$

$$
m = 4 \times 10^{-27} \ \text{kg}
$$

$$
B = 0.5 \ \text{T}
$$

$$
v = 10^6 \ \text{m/s}
$$

We need to find the magnitude of the Lorentz force.

---

## Lorentz Force Formula

The magnetic part of the Lorentz force is:

$$
F = qvB\sin\theta
$$

where:

- $F$ is the magnetic force
- $q$ is the charge
- $v$ is the speed of the particle
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength
- $\theta$ is the angle between velocity and magnetic field

---

## Angle Between Velocity and Magnetic Field

The particle moves perpendicular to the magnetic field.

So:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

and:

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

Therefore:

$$
F = qvB
$$

---

## Substitute the Values

$$
F = (2 \times 10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)
$$

First multiply:

$$
2 \times 0.5 = 1
$$

So:

$$
F = 1 \times 10^{-19} \times 10^6
$$

$$
F = 1 \times 10^{-13} \ \text{N}
$$

---

## Final Answer

The magnitude of the Lorentz force is:

$$
\boxed{F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13} \ \text{N}}
$$
