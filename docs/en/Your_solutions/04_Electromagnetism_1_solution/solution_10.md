# 10. Lorentz Force Acting on a Wire

A straight wire has length:

$$
L = 2.0 \ \text{m}
$$

The current in the wire is:

$$
I = 10 \ \text{A}
$$

The magnetic field strength is:

$$
B = 0.5 \ \text{T}
$$

We need to calculate the magnetic force on the wire for different angles.

---

## Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

The magnetic force on a wire carrying current in a magnetic field is:

$$
F = BIL\sin\theta
$$

where:

- $F$ is the magnetic force
- $B$ is the magnetic field strength
- $I$ is the current
- $L$ is the length of the wire
- $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field

---

## Substitute the Common Values

First calculate:

$$
BIL = (0.5)(10)(2.0)
$$

$$
BIL = 10 \ \text{N}
$$

So the force becomes:

$$
F = 10\sin\theta
$$

---

# a) Angle $\theta = 90^\circ$

For:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

we know:

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

Therefore:

$$
F = 10(1)
$$

$$
F = 10 \ \text{N}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{F = 10 \ \text{N}}
$$

---

# b) Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$

For:

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

we know:

$$
\sin 45^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707
$$

Therefore:

$$
F = 10(0.707)
$$

$$
F \approx 7.07 \ \text{N}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{F \approx 7.07 \ \text{N}}
$$

---

# c) Angle $\theta = 0^\circ$

For:

$$
\theta = 0^\circ
$$

we know:

$$
\sin 0^\circ = 0
$$

Therefore:

$$
F = 10(0)
$$

$$
F = 0 \ \text{N}
$$

So:

$$
\boxed{F = 0 \ \text{N}}
$$

---

# Final Answers

$$
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c|c}
\theta & F \\
\hline
90^\circ & 10 \ \text{N} \\
45^\circ & 7.07 \ \text{N} \\
0^\circ & 0 \ \text{N}
\end{array}
}
$$

---

## Conclusion

The force is maximum when the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field:

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

The force is zero when the wire is parallel to the magnetic field:

$$
\theta = 0^\circ
$$
