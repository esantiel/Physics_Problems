# 5. Field Levitation

We need to find the electric field strength required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity.

A proton has:

$$
m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \ \text{kg}
$$

$$
e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \ \text{C}
$$

The gravitational acceleration at Earth's surface is:

$$
g = 9.8 \ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## Forces Acting on the Proton

There are two main forces:

1. Gravitational force downward
2. Electric force upward

For levitation, the proton must stay in equilibrium.

So:

$$
F_e = F_g
$$

---

## Gravitational Force

The gravitational force is:

$$
F_g = mg
$$

For the proton:

$$
F_g = m_p g
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F_g = (1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)
$$

$$
F_g \approx 1.64 \times 10^{-26} \ \text{N}
$$

---

## Electric Force

The electric force on a charge in an electric field is:

$$
F_e = qE
$$

For a proton:

$$
F_e = eE
$$

For levitation:

$$
eE = m_p g
$$

Solve for $E$:

$$
E = \frac{m_p g}{e}
$$

---

## Calculate the Electric Field

Substitute the values:

$$
E =
\frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}
{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \ \text{N/C}
$$

Since:

$$
1 \ \text{N/C} = 1 \ \text{V/m}
$$

we can also write:

$$
E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \ \text{V/m}
$$

---

## Direction of the Electric Field

A proton has a positive charge.

The electric force on a positive charge is in the same direction as the electric field.

Since the electric force must be upward to balance gravity, the electric field must point upward.

---

# Final Answer

The required electric field strength is:

$$
\boxed{E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \ \text{N/C}}
$$

or:

$$
\boxed{E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \ \text{V/m}}
$$

The direction of the electric field is:

$$
\boxed{\text{upward}}
$$
