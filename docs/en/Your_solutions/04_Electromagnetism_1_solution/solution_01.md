# 1. Coulomb's Law

Four point charges of:

$$
+1.0 \ \text{C}
$$

are placed at the corners of a square.

The side length of the square is:

$$
a = 1.0 \ \text{m}
$$

A charge of:

$$
q = -2.0 \ \text{C}
$$

is placed at the center of the square.

We need to calculate the **magnitude** and **direction** of the electric force on the center charge.

---

## Coulomb's Law

The electric force between two point charges is given by:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

where:

- $F$ is the electric force
- $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2$
- $q_1$ and $q_2$ are the charges
- $r$ is the distance between the charges

---

## Distance from the Center to Each Corner

The charge is at the center of the square.

The distance from the center to each corner is half of the diagonal of the square.

The diagonal of a square is:

$$
d = a\sqrt{2}
$$

So the distance from the center to one corner is:

$$
r = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

Since:

$$
a = 1.0 \ \text{m}
$$

we get:

$$
r = \frac{1.0\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

$$
r = 0.707 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Force from One Corner Charge

Using Coulomb's law:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F = (9.0 \times 10^9) \frac{|(1.0)(-2.0)|}{(0.707)^2}
$$

Since:

$$
(0.707)^2 \approx 0.5
$$

we get:

$$
F = (9.0 \times 10^9) \frac{2.0}{0.5}
$$

$$
F = 3.6 \times 10^{10} \ \text{N}
$$

So, each corner charge attracts the center charge with a force of:

$$
3.6 \times 10^{10} \ \text{N}
$$

---

## Direction of the Forces

The center charge is negative:

$$
-2.0 \ \text{C}
$$

The corner charges are positive:

$$
+1.0 \ \text{C}
$$

Therefore, each force is **attractive**.

This means each positive charge pulls the negative charge toward its own corner.

However, the four positive charges are placed symmetrically around the center.

So:

- the force toward the top-right corner is canceled by the force toward the bottom-left corner
- the force toward the top-left corner is canceled by the force toward the bottom-right corner

Because of symmetry, all forces cancel each other.

---

## Net Force

Therefore, the net electric force on the charge at the center is:

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 0
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{F_{\text{net}} = 0 \ \text{N}}
$$

The magnitude of the electric force is:

$$
\boxed{0 \ \text{N}}
$$

The direction is:

$$
\boxed{\text{No direction, because the net force is zero}}
$$
