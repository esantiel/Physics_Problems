# 4. Force Comparison

We need to calculate:

1. The **electric force** between an electron and a proton
2. The **gravitational force** between them
3. The ratio:

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g}
$$

The average distance between the electron and proton in a hydrogen atom is:

$$
r = 5.3 \times 10^{-11} \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Given Values

Charge of electron and proton:

$$
e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \ \text{C}
$$

Mass of electron:

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \ \text{kg}
$$

Mass of proton:

$$
m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \ \text{kg}
$$

Coulomb constant:

$$
k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2
$$

Gravitational constant:

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \ \text{N m}^2/\text{kg}^2
$$

---

# Electric Force

The electric force is calculated using Coulomb's law:

$$
F_e = k \frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}
$$

For an electron and a proton:

$$
q_1 = q_2 = e
$$

So:

$$
F_e = k \frac{e^2}{r^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F_e =
(9.0 \times 10^9)
\frac{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

First calculate:

$$
(1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2 = 2.56 \times 10^{-38}
$$

and:

$$
(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2 = 2.809 \times 10^{-21}
$$

Therefore:

$$
F_e =
(9.0 \times 10^9)
\frac{2.56 \times 10^{-38}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}
$$

$$
F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \ \text{N}
$$

---

# Gravitational Force

The gravitational force is calculated using Newton's law of gravitation:

$$
F_g = G \frac{m_em_p}{r^2}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
F_g =
(6.67 \times 10^{-11})
\frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

First calculate:

$$
(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})
\approx 1.52 \times 10^{-57}
$$

So:

$$
F_g =
(6.67 \times 10^{-11})
\frac{1.52 \times 10^{-57}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}
$$

$$
F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \ \text{N}
$$

---

# Ratio of Electric Force to Gravitational Force

Now calculate:

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g}
=
\frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}
$$

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g}
\approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}
$$

---

# Final Answer

The magnitude of the electric force is:

$$
\boxed{F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \ \text{N}}
$$

The magnitude of the gravitational force is:

$$
\boxed{F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \ \text{N}}
$$

The ratio is:

$$
\boxed{\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}}
$$

---

## Conclusion

The electric force between the electron and proton is about:

$$
2.3 \times 10^{39}
$$

times stronger than the gravitational force.

So, in atoms, the electric force is much more important than gravity.
