# 2. Electric Potential

Point charges of:

$$
+1 \ \text{C}, \quad -2 \ \text{C}, \quad +3 \ \text{C}, \quad -4 \ \text{C}
$$

are placed at the corners of a square.

The side length of the square is:

$$
a = 1.0 \ \text{m}
$$

We need to calculate the **electric potential at the center** of the square.

---

## Electric Potential Formula

The electric potential due to a point charge is:

$$
V = k \frac{q}{r}
$$

where:

- $V$ is the electric potential
- $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \ \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2$
- $q$ is the charge
- $r$ is the distance from the charge to the point

For multiple charges, electric potential is added algebraically:

$$
V_{\text{total}} = k \left( \frac{q_1}{r} + \frac{q_2}{r} + \frac{q_3}{r} + \frac{q_4}{r} \right)
$$

Since all charges are at the same distance from the center:

$$
V_{\text{total}} = \frac{k}{r}(q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4)
$$

---

## Distance from Center to Each Corner

The distance from the center of the square to each corner is half of the diagonal.

The diagonal of the square is:

$$
d = a\sqrt{2}
$$

So:

$$
r = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

Substitute:

$$
a = 1.0 \ \text{m}
$$

$$
r = \frac{1.0\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

$$
r = 0.707 \ \text{m}
$$

---

## Sum of the Charges

The charges are:

$$
q_1 = +1 \ \text{C}
$$

$$
q_2 = -2 \ \text{C}
$$

$$
q_3 = +3 \ \text{C}
$$

$$
q_4 = -4 \ \text{C}
$$

Now add them:

$$
q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4 = 1 - 2 + 3 - 4
$$

$$
q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4 = -2 \ \text{C}
$$

---

## Calculate the Total Electric Potential

Using:

$$
V_{\text{total}} = \frac{k}{r}(q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4)
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
V_{\text{total}} = \frac{9.0 \times 10^9}{0.707}(-2)
$$

$$
V_{\text{total}} \approx -2.55 \times 10^{10} \ \text{V}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{V_{\text{center}} \approx -2.55 \times 10^{10} \ \text{V}}
$$

The electric potential at the center of the square is:

$$
\boxed{-2.55 \times 10^{10} \ \text{volts}}
$$

---

## Note

Electric potential is a **scalar quantity**, not a vector.

Therefore, we do not need to consider direction. We only add the charge values algebraically.
