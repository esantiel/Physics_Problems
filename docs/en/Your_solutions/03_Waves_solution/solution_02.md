## 2. String Harmonics

A guitar string has:

$$
L = 64 \text{ cm} = 0.64 \text{ m}
$$

and a fundamental frequency of:

$$
f_1 = 330 \text{ Hz}
$$

For the fundamental mode (one antinode), the string length is half of the wavelength:

$$
L = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

So the wavelength is:

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

$$
\lambda = 2(0.64)
$$

$$
\lambda = 1.28 \text{ m}
$$

Now use the wave equation:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

$$
v = 330 \times 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Answer

$$
v = 422.4 \text{ m/s}
$$
