# 1. Wave Properties

A sound wave in air has a frequency of **440 Hz**.  
The speed of sound is **343 m/s** in air and **1482 m/s** in water.

The formula for wavelength is:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

where:

- $\lambda$ = wavelength
- $v$ = wave speed
- $f$ = frequency

---

## Wavelength in Air

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.7795 \, \text{m}
$$

So, the wavelength in air is:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## Wavelength in Water

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.368 \, \text{m}
$$

So, the wavelength in water is:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{m}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78 \, \text{m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \, \text{m}
$$
