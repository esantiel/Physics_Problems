## 6. Wave Equation

The wave is given by:

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

We compare this with the standard form of a wave:

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

where:

- $A$ = amplitude
- $k$ = wave number
- $\omega$ = angular frequency

From the given equation:

$$
A = 0.05
$$

$$
k = 2\pi
$$

$$
\omega = 50\pi
$$

---

### a) Amplitude $A$

The amplitude is the coefficient in front of the sine function:

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

---

### b) Wavelength $\lambda$

We use:

$$
k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Substitute $k = 2\pi$:

$$
2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

So:

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

---

### c) Frequency $f$

We use:

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

Substitute $\omega = 50\pi$:

$$
50\pi = 2\pi f
$$

Divide both sides by $2\pi$:

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

---

### d) Wave speed $v$

We use:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
v = 25 \times 1
$$

$$
v = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## Final Answers

### a) Amplitude

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

### b) Wavelength

$$
\lambda = 1 \text{ m}
$$

### c) Frequency

$$
f = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

### d) Wave speed

$$
v = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$
