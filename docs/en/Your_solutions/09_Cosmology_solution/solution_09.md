# 9. Cosmic Expansion

Distance:

$$
d=100\times10^6\ \text{ly}
$$

Convert to Mpc:

$$
d=\frac{100}{3.26}
\approx30.7\ \text{Mpc}
$$

Recession speed:

$$
v=H_0d
$$

$$
v=70(30.7)
$$

$$
v\approx2147\ \text{km/s}
$$

Time:

$$
t=10^9\ \text{years}
$$

Convert to seconds:

$$
t\approx3.156\times10^{16}\ \text{s}
$$

Increase in distance:

$$
\Delta d=vt
$$

$$
\Delta d\approx6.78\times10^{19}\ \text{km}
$$

Convert to light-years:

$$
\Delta d\approx7.2\times10^6\ \text{ly}
$$

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{7.2\ \text{million light-years}}
$$
